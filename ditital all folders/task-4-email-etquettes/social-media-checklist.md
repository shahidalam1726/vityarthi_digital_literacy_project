# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 4 | Rishab Jain | 25BCE10989

---

## ✅ DO's

1. **Keep your profiles professional and up to date.**
   Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and keep your bio relevant to your academic
   and career interests. Employers and internship coordinators frequently review
   social media profiles.

2. **Think before you post.**
   Always pause and consider whether a post, comment, or share could be misinterpreted,
   cause offence, or reflect poorly on you professionally — even years from now.

3. **Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).**
   Protect all your accounts with 2FA so that even if your password is compromised,
   unauthorized access is prevented.

4. **Report harmful or suspicious content.**
   If you encounter cyberbullying, fake accounts, scams, or any threatening content,
   use the platform's reporting tools or contact cybercrime.gov.in / Helpline 1930.

5. **Respect others' privacy and consent.**
   Always ask for permission before posting photos or videos that include other people.
   Never share someone else's personal information without their explicit consent.

6. **Verify information before sharing.**
   Cross-check news, statistics, or claims from at least two reliable sources before
   sharing. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — can cause real harm.

7. **Separate personal and professional content.**
   Consider having different audiences or accounts for casual personal posts and
   professional networking. LinkedIn should always remain strictly professional.

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

1. **Do not share personal identifying information publicly.**
   Avoid posting your phone number, home address, date of birth, college ID number,
   or Aadhaar/PAN details on any public platform.

2. **Do not engage with strangers offering jobs, prizes, or financial help.**
   Unsolicited messages offering money, prizes, or remote jobs on social media are
   almost always phishing attempts or scams. Ignore and report them.

3. **Do not post in anger or frustration.**
   Never vent publicly about professors, classmates, companies, or institutions during
   a moment of frustration. Such posts can go viral and permanently harm your
   reputation.

4. **Do not click on unknown links sent via DMs or comments.**
   Malicious links in direct messages or comments can steal your login credentials,
   install malware, or redirect you to phishing websites.

5. **Do not use social media during examinations or while driving.**
   Beyond being unsafe, posting during exams can be used as evidence of academic
   misconduct if content is related to the exam.
