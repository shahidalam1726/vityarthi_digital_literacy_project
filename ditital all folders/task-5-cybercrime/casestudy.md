## What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which an attacker unlawfully collects and uses
another person's personal information — such as their name, Aadhaar number, PAN card
details, bank credentials, or date of birth — to impersonate them for financial gain,
fraudulent applications, or other criminal purposes.

---

## Case Study: The Case of Aryan – A College Student Targeted Online

Aryan, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Pune, was actively using
multiple online platforms after joining college. He had filled out several online forms
for scholarships and college portals, and had publicly listed his full name, date of
birth, college name, and phone number on his social media profiles.

**How it happened — step by step:**

1. **Data collection:** An attacker identified Aryan's publicly visible social media
   profile and harvested basic details: full name, date of birth, city, and college.

2. **Phishing attack:** The attacker sent Aryan a convincing email impersonating his
   college's scholarship portal, asking him to "verify his identity" by entering his
   Aadhaar number and bank account details on a fake website that looked identical to
   the official portal.

3. **Credential capture:** Aryan, believing it was legitimate, entered his details.
   The attacker now had his Aadhaar number, mobile number, and partial bank details.

4. **Account takeover:** Using the Aadhaar number and date of birth, the attacker
   was able to reset Aryan's e-banking password by exploiting weak security questions.

5. **Financial fraud:** Within 48 hours, small transactions were made from Aryan's
   account. A loan application was also submitted in his name using his stolen identity.

**Who is usually targeted?**
Young students and first-time internet users are prime targets because they tend to
share more information publicly, are less aware of phishing tactics, and have newly
created financial accounts with weaker security settings.

**Consequences:**
Aryan spent over three months filing complaints with his bank, the National Cyber
Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in), and the cybercrime helpline (1930) to recover his
account and get the fraudulent loan reversed. The incident caused significant
financial stress, loss of trust in digital platforms, and a temporary impact on his
academic focus.
