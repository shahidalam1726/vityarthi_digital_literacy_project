# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Task 5 | Rishab Jain | 25BCE10989
*Tailored for college students in India*

---

## 🔐 Account & Password Safety

1. **Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
   A strong password is at least 12 characters long and combines uppercase letters,
   lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across
   multiple platforms.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all important accounts.**
   Turn on 2FA for your college email, banking apps, UPI apps (Google Pay, PhonePe,
   Paytm), and social media. This adds a second layer of verification beyond just
   your password.

3. **Never share your OTP, PIN, or password with anyone — ever.**
   No legitimate bank, app, or institution will ever ask for your OTP over a phone
   call, SMS, or email. If someone asks for it, it is a scam.

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Specific to Indian Students)

4. **Never scan a QR code or enter your UPI PIN to receive money.**
   UPI PINs and QR code scans are used only to SEND money, not to receive it.
   Anyone asking you to "scan this to get a refund" is attempting fraud.

5. **Verify UPI IDs and bank account numbers carefully before every transfer.**
   Always double-check the recipient's name that appears after entering a UPI ID
   before confirming a payment. Fraudsters use IDs that look similar to genuine ones.

6. **Never click on links in SMS messages claiming to be from your bank or UPI app.**
   Go directly to your bank's official website or app. Report suspicious bank-related
   SMS messages to 1930 or cybercrime.gov.in immediately.

---

## 🌐 Online Privacy & Social Media Safety

7. **Limit what personal information you share publicly on social media.**
   Keep your phone number, date of birth, home address, college ID, and Aadhaar
   details off all public-facing profiles. Attackers use these fragments to build a
   complete identity profile.

8. **Check and update your privacy settings regularly.**
   On Instagram, Facebook, and other platforms, set your profile to "Friends Only"
   or "Private." Review which apps have access to your data in your phone settings.

---

## 🎣 Phishing & Scam Awareness

9. **Verify the sender's email address carefully before clicking any link.**
   Official emails from colleges, banks, or government portals will never be sent from
   Gmail, Yahoo, or Hotmail addresses. Check for misspellings like "vit-bhopaal.ac.in".

10. **If you suspect you have been scammed, act immediately.**
    Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline **1930** (available 24x7) or file a complaint
    at **cybercrime.gov.in**. Reporting quickly increases the chances of recovering
    lost funds and preventing further damage.
